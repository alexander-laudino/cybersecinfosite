Information Security Positions

Chief Security Officer – usually an executive position, this role encompasses both physical and information security, experience required includes hands-on security roles and administrative roles as this individual has to have the entire scope of the organization covered from a security perspective. Plans and designs company’s security program to protect the operations of the organization. Usually liaisons with law enforcement agencies and handles all physical security of personnel.

Chief Information Security Officer – non-executive management position, this role is recently being looked at by the SEC as a necessary requirement in all boardrooms. This role is specifically focused on the information security program of the organization and reports to either the CSO or the CIO. An individual in this role can expect to manage the infosec program, draft and approve security policies, and works with the CIO on strategic planning to further develop operation plans with their team. In this role, an individual must have an in-depth understanding of core technologies as well as tech coming down the pipeline as they handle all acquisition and securing of the organization’s information technology infrastructure.

Security Manager – this role supports the day-to-day operations of the information security program. In this position, an individual develops, manages, and monitors the performance of the infosec programs and control systems as directed by the CISO. This role includes management responsibilities such as scheduling and preparing budgets and doesn’t require a deeper technical proficiency on specific technologies. With that in mind, they also prepare risk assessments and ensure effective risk management controls are in place. Represents infosec during organization change management processes and handles incident response and disaster recovery in addition to overseeing a team of security analysts.

Security Analyst – this role is where the pedal meets the pavement. Various roles can fall under this such as technicians, architects, and engineers as they all play an important role in designing, building, managing, and enforcing the organizations information security program. Handles deployment of firewall and IDPS systems to ensure the organization’s information technology infrastructure is available and secure when needed by any of the organization’s personnel.
